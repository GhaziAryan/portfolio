Selected work.

Process over outcomes. Here's how I actually think through problems.

Capital One

Rebuilding how a bank does risk assessments

300+ annual assessments, fragmented across tools, with no shared language. I led the build of a platform to standardize the whole thing — starting from an empty backlog.

The real challenge

It wasn't a tech problem. It was a people problem. Risk teams had their own workflows, their own terminology, their own tools. Getting alignment meant building personal relationships first, then leveraging them to drive adoption. I time-blocked aggressively — half the week on discovery and stakeholder conversations, half on product execution.

What shipped

  • Question Bank MVP — questionnaire-as-a-service
  • Scoping module for assessment planning
  • Diagnostics recommender
  • Standardized BPMN workflows via Camunda

How I work

I talked to users obsessively. Defined testable success criteria before a line of code was written. Ran agile squads of 6-8 engineers, partnered with design, and ruthlessly prioritized. Evangelized the platform to 100+ stakeholders — 50+ teams expressed qualified interest, which shaped the multi-quarter roadmap.

What it taught me

Enterprise adoption is earned through trust, not decks. Also — mentoring three associate PMs through this made my own thinking sharper. Teaching is underrated as a PM skill.

Side project — founder

Tape: an AI trading journal that actually works

A mobile app that runs an LLM on your phone to turn messy trade reflections into structured behavioral insights. Built with AI. Powered by AI. Used by me.

The problem I had

I wanted to journal my trades, but every app felt like filling out a form. I wanted to just type — stream of consciousness, emotional, honest — and have something smart extract the patterns. So I built it.

The hard part

Getting real value from a local LLM. The input field had to feel completely free-form while the model quietly structured everything behind the scenes. Lots of prompt fine-tuning and guardrail building to keep inference fast and output useful.

This is the part that matters to me: Tape is built with AI, for AI features, using AI tools. Claude Code for dev, Cursor for scaffolding, ChatGPT for discovery. It's not a side project — it's my thesis on where PM is going.
